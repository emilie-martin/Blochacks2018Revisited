CV Template Generator

Webpage overview

This webpage was created as a way to facilitate the creation of a ciriculum vitae (CV) for job applications.
It targets anyone looking for a job, be it a student or a migrant unfamiliar with the employment process here in Montreal.

 Written and Spoken Languages 

List the languages you have a good command of and specify, in parentheses, where it applies to the written or spoken language. You can also add any additional language you know and/or aren't perfectly fluent in, so long as it is pertinent and you at least know the basics.

Example:

  • English (spoken and written)
  • French (spoken)
  • Spanish (basic)

 Education 

From most to least recent, list your educational background.

Format:
    School name/establishment    |    Program name    |    Start date    |    End date

Example:
    Best University    |    Software Engineering    |    09-04-2018    |    05-25-2022

Enter any relevant extracurriculars here, from most to least recent.

 Work Experience 

From most to least recent, list your previous work experiences as well as a short description of what you have worked on.

Guidelines:

  • Make sure you use a recognizable title name like doctor, science teacher, data analyst, consultant, etc.
  • List tasks and projects you have completed that are relevant to the position you are trying to apply for.
  • Use the past tense while talking about the projects you have worked on.

Format:
   Company name    |    Position name    |    Start date    |    End date
    A short list of tasks and projects you have completed and worked on

Example:
   Amazing Company    |    Graphic designer    |    Start date    |    End date    

  • Drafted concept art for CoolGame
  • Worked on cover art for John Smith (Pearson)'s book
